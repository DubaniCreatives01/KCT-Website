About KCT Trust

Empowering the Khayelitsha community through strategic social, residential, and commercial development.

Our History

City Founding & Land Agreements

KCT was founded by the City of Cape Town in 2003 to develop commercial, residential and community facilities in Khayelitsha. These developments help generate economic opportunities and uplift the lives of residents.

The Trust, previously a municipal entity, now trades as a non-profit organisation. A Land Availability Agreement (LAA) entered into between the City and KCT allows KCT to assist with the KBD development and hold equity in associated developments, the returns of which are used in other community developments.

"Empowering the community through key infrastructure, training directories, and equity opportunities."

Corporate Mission

KCT's mission is to advance the KBD by developing commercial, residential, and communal facilities.

Future Vision

KCT's vision is to empower the Khayelitsha community through the promotion of social and economic development.

Core Values

Openness, integrity, sound corporate governance, and community transparency in all planning actions.

Our Strength

Key Value Proposition

  • Strategic Partnerships with key municipal and financial stakeholders.
  • Community Empowerment: Residents benefit directly from all structural improvements.
  • Multi-Sectoral Impact: Commercial and housing initiatives yield immediate dividends.
  • KDF Elected Representation on the board ensures community voice structures.
  • LAA rate benefits: Preferential rates giving secure access to land portfolios.
Our Targets

KCT Objectives

  • To resolve LAA matters with City of Cape Town to enable planned operations.
  • To provide affordable housing that meets the preference and pricing of residents.
  • To capacitate KCT and its commercial arms to plan and implement projects.
  • To improve the retail offering and functionality of the KCT Mall.
  • To implement human capital programs to augment local business skills.
Strategic Alignment

How KCT's Value is Measured

We align our developmental objectives with the five pillars of the City of Cape Town's Integrated Development Plan (IDP) to alleviate specific Khayelitsha needs:

Khayelitsha Needs Alleviation

  • Addressing spatial inefficiency
  • Improving available facilities & security
  • Creating equity participation & ownership
  • Promoting socio-economic growth
  • Ensuring jobs, training, & learning directories
